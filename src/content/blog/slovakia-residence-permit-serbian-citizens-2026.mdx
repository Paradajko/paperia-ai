# Slovakia Residence Permit for Serbian Citizens in 2026

Moving from Serbia to Slovakia can feel administratively manageable because the countries are geographically close and many Serbian citizens already travel to the Schengen area without a short-stay visa. That familiarity can be misleading. A visa-free visit is not permission to live or work in Slovakia. If your real purpose is employment, study, family reunion, business, or research, you normally need the correct Slovak residence route and documents supporting that exact purpose.

This guide gives you a practical starting point for preparing a temporary residence application in 2026. Requirements depend on your personal situation and can change, so verify the final checklist with the Slovak embassy, the Foreign Police, or a licensed immigration professional before filing.

## Why move from Serbia to Slovakia?

Slovakia attracts Serbian applicants for several practical reasons. It is close enough for relatively easy travel home, belongs to the EU and Schengen area, and has employers recruiting in manufacturing, logistics, technology, construction, health care, and services. Slovak universities also offer study programmes that may lead to a temporary residence route. Family links, business opportunities, and research placements create other legitimate pathways.

Serbian biometric passport holders can generally make visa-free short visits to the Schengen area for up to 90 days in any 180-day period. The rule covers short visits; it does not itself authorize employment or long-term residence. Track all Schengen days carefully, because time spent in other Schengen countries usually counts toward the same limit.

## Main residence routes

Slovak temporary residence is granted for a specific documented purpose. The main routes relevant to Serbian citizens include:

### Employment

Employment is the most common route for many applicants. The process may involve an employer reporting a vacancy and the Labour Office confirming that it can be filled by a third-country national. The exact sequence depends on whether you use a single-permit procedure, a work permit route, seasonal employment, or the EU Blue Card for qualifying highly skilled work. Do not assume that a signed job offer alone completes the residence process.

### Study

You may apply on the basis of admission to a recognized Slovak school or university. The admission document is central evidence of purpose. Students can have different documentary exemptions and shorter decision periods in some cases, but they still need to check passport, criminal-record, accommodation, insurance, and post-arrival obligations applicable to them.

### Family reunion

Family reunion may be available to a spouse, dependent child, or another qualifying family member of a person with residence in Slovakia. Marriage certificates, birth certificates, evidence of the sponsor’s residence, and sometimes dependency or parental-consent evidence may be required. Family facts that look obvious privately still need formal public documents.

### Business

Business residence can cover activity as a sole trader or through a Slovak company. It usually requires a credible business purpose and proof of financial resources for both residence and business activity. A first business-residence application generally cannot be submitted at the Foreign Police simply because the applicant entered Slovakia visa-free; applicants should check the current embassy filing rule before making travel plans.

### Research and development

Researchers normally document their purpose with a hosting agreement from an eligible organization. Research residence can have different processing and documentary rules from ordinary employment. Confirm whether your planned activity is genuinely research and development or another special activity.

## Documents to prepare

A standard temporary-residence file typically starts with the official application form, a valid passport, proof of the residence purpose, criminal-record evidence, and proof of accommodation. Financial evidence is especially important for business applications and may be relevant elsewhere depending on the route.

For employment, supporting evidence may include an employment contract, a binding promise of employment, or documents connected with the single-permit process. For study, use the formal admission confirmation. Family applicants should prepare civil-status documents. Researchers generally need a hosting agreement.

Accommodation evidence causes frequent problems. Depending on the case, acceptable proof may be a tenancy agreement, accommodation-facility confirmation, proof of ownership, or a properly executed affidavit from the person providing housing. The document must cover the required period and identify the property and parties clearly.

A medical report is usually a post-approval obligation rather than a document filed with every initial application. When required, it must come from an authorized Slovak medical facility and confirm that the applicant does not suffer from a disease threatening public health. Health-insurance evidence also has route-specific timing and exemptions. Follow the instructions attached to your residence decision instead of buying random insurance or medical certificates too early.

## Apostilles and official translations

Documents issued in Serbia for use in Slovakia often require authentication and an official translation. The general Slovak rule is that a foreign public document must be authenticated by apostille or consular superlegalization unless an applicable international agreement creates an exemption.

For a Serbian criminal-record extract, birth certificate, marriage certificate, diploma, or similar public document, confirm whether the specific document requires an apostille and which Serbian authority issues it. Do this before translation: the apostille itself may also need to be translated.

Slovak authorities generally require an official translation into Slovak, with Czech accepted in specified situations. A translation made abroad can require additional confirmation that it was produced by an authorized person. The safest practical approach is often to use a translator registered for official Slovak translations and to provide the translator with the complete authenticated document, including stamps and attachments.

## Where to apply and how long it takes

An application is normally filed in person at the Slovak embassy accredited for the country that issued your passport or for your country of residence. In eligible cases, an applicant who is lawfully in Slovakia and does not need a visa to enter may apply at the competent Foreign Police department. Important exceptions apply. For example, the first business-residence application generally cannot use this visa-free in-country option.

Book appointments early and do not rely on being able to file immediately after arriving. A file is considered complete only when the authority accepts the application and required attachments. Many foreign-issued supporting documents must generally be no older than 90 days, so ordering everything too early can also create a problem.

Decision periods vary by route. The general statutory period can be up to 90 days. A single-permit employment case may have a 60-day period, while complete applications for study, research and development, and certain other categories may fall under a 30-day period. These are legal decision periods, not guaranteed end-to-end timelines. Appointment availability, Labour Office steps, incomplete evidence, delivery of the residence card, and post-approval obligations can make the full process longer.

After approval, pay attention to entry, address-reporting, insurance, and medical-report deadlines. Missing a post-approval step can create trouble even after the main residence decision is positive.

## Common mistakes

The first common mistake is submitting a Serbian document without the required apostille or other authentication. The second is translating only the main page while leaving the apostille, stamps, or attachments untranslated. A normal commercial translation is not automatically an official translation accepted by Slovak authorities.

Another frequent problem is weak accommodation evidence: an unsigned contract, the wrong property owner, insufficient duration, or missing proof that the provider may use the property. Applicants also use criminal-record documents that are too old or valid only for part of a country.

Employment applicants sometimes assume the employer handles everything. Confirm who reports the vacancy, who supplies the employment document, and when you are legally allowed to start work. Visa-free presence does not equal work authorization.

Finally, avoid treating a generic online list as your final checklist. Your route, age, previous countries of residence, family situation, and place of filing can change the required evidence.

## Practical next step

Choose the residence purpose first. Then build a dated document plan working backwards from your intended filing appointment. Mark which documents need Serbian issuance, apostille, official Slovak translation, employer or school cooperation, and recent issue dates. Keep scans, but bring originals where required.
